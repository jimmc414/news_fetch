Germany is preparing for a general election to the Bundestag, the lower house of its parliament, on 23 February, after its “traffic-light” coalition of social democrats, liberals and greens collapsed. The country’s electoral system is highly proportional, so polls give a good indication of what shape of government might be possible after the election. Who’s who? Party profiles The outgoing Bundestag It’s clear from the makeup of the current chamber that Scholz’s government could not survive without the FDP, but would also struggle to form any new coalition that did not include the conservative CDU/CSU. For their part, Friedrich Merz’s party currently have more to gain from new elections than from propping up the chancellor.
